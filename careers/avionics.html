Avionics Technician

A career that elevates

Overview

Avionics Technicians specialize in the electronic systems used in aircraft, including navigation, communication, radar, and autopilot systems. They install, test, and repair complex systems to ensure safe and efficient flight operations.

Eligibility

  • 10+2 with Physics & Mathematics
  • Diploma/Degree in Electronics or Avionics Engineering
  • DGCA-approved training for certification
  • Strong problem-solving and technical skills

Training Path

Usually a 2–3 year program covering avionics systems, followed by hands-on training in certified MRO facilities or avionics labs.

Career Opportunities

Work in airline MROs, aircraft manufacturing companies, defense sectors, or avionics development firms.

Expected Salary

Starting salary ranges from ₹30,000–₹50,000/month; certified and experienced professionals can earn ₹1 lakh or more.
