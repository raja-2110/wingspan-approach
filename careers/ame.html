Aircraft Maintenance Engineer

A career that elevates

Overview

AMEs ensure that aircraft are airworthy and safe to fly. They perform routine maintenance, troubleshooting, and certify aircraft before every flight.

Eligibility

  • Passed 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry & Maths
  • Must complete DGCA-approved AME course
  • Class 2 Medical Certificate
  • Strong technical & analytical skills

Training Path

A 2-year academic program followed by 2 years of practical experience in an MRO (Maintenance, Repair and Overhaul) organization.

Career Opportunities

Jobs in airlines, MROs, aircraft manufacturing companies, and regulatory bodies like DGCA.

Expected Salary

Entry-level salary is ₹30,000–₹50,000 per month. Licensed AMEs can earn ₹1–2 lakh/month or more.
